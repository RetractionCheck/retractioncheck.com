Get in Touch

At RetractionCheck, we are committed to ensuring the accuracy and reliability of Retractioncheck. We value user feedback and encourage researchers, academics, and publishers for collaboration opportunities or to report any issues or suggest improvements.

Reporting Issues (False Positives/Negatives)

Please let us know if you encounter a false positive (an article incorrectly flagged as retracted) or a false negative (a retracted article not identified by our system). We rely on user feedback to continuously refine our service and improve accuracy.

  1. To report an issue, email us at bijuvv@shcollege.ac.in with details of the DOI, article title, and the error encountered.
  2. You can also submit corrections via our Feedback Form on the website.

Collaboration & Research Partnerships

We welcome collaboration proposals from researchers, academic institutions, and database providers interested in improving retraction awareness and research integrity. If you are a journal editor, librarian, or developer looking to integrate our tool into your workflow, please email collaborate@retractioncheck.com to discuss partnership opportunities.
